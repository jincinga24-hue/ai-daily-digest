# About The Daily AI Shift

**The Daily AI Shift** is a daily AI news blog for Australian chemical engineers and ChemE students — tracking how artificial intelligence is reshaping process engineering careers and industries in Australia.

Every day, it covers the most significant AI developments in chemical engineering and connects them to real-world impact on Australian ChemE careers, tools, and industries.

## Who It's For

- **ChemE students** at Australian universities — deciding which skills to build, whether AI will reshape your chosen specialisation, and which employers are leading adoption. Relevant if you're studying at Melbourne, UNSW, Monash, UQ, Adelaide, Curtin, or RMIT.
- **Early-career chemical engineers** (0–5 years) working in process design, mining, oil and gas, water treatment, or pharmaceutical manufacturing — navigating how AI is changing daily practice
- **Mid-career professionals** in Australian resources, water, and pharmaceutical sectors who want to understand which AI tools matter for their role and industry
- Anyone working in or adjacent to Australian chemical engineering who wants to understand where AI is heading and what it means for their field

## What It Covers

- Daily AI news filtered for chemical engineering relevance — process simulation tools, digital twins, lab automation, predictive maintenance, safety systems
- Career and industry analysis grounded in the Australian ChemE job market
- An **AI Impact Radar** showing theoretical vs observed AI coverage across 12 Australian ChemE sub-sectors — from process design and mining to pharmaceutical manufacturing and water treatment
- Curated learning resources (articles, videos, GitHub repos) linked to each day's news — sourced from IChemE, Engineers Australia, RACI, AspenTech, AVEVA, and Australian university ChemE departments

## Industries Covered

- Mining and minerals processing (BHP, Rio Tinto, Fortescue — Pilbara and beyond)
- Oil and gas refining (Woodside, Santos, Beach Energy)
- Water and wastewater treatment (SA Water, Melbourne Water, Yarra Valley Water)
- Pharmaceutical manufacturing and biotech (CSL, Pfizer Puurs, AstraZeneca)
- Food and beverage processing
- Industrial chemicals and explosives (Orica, Incitec Pivot)
- Environmental and sustainability engineering

## Why Chemical Engineering?

Chemical engineering is at a genuine inflection point. AI is being embedded directly into the tools process engineers use every day — Aspen Plus, HYSYS, AVEVA, UniSim — not just as an add-on, but as a co-pilot for flowsheet generation, energy optimisation, and troubleshooting. Autonomous operations are expanding in Australian mining. Bioprocess AI is accelerating drug development timelines. HAZOP and safety analysis are being augmented by AI document review.

The global AI conversation rarely addresses these specifics. The Daily AI Shift covers them directly, with an Australian lens.

## Spelling and Style

All content uses **Australian English**: organisation, colour, programme, analyse, labour, optimise, modelling, licence (noun), practise (verb), and so on.
